Aglio e Olio

No two spaghetti aglio e olio recipes are alike, but this one is pretty true to the classic method. The key is slowly toasting the garlic slices to a perfect golden-brown in the olive oil. If it's too light, you don't get the full flavor and if it's too dark it gets bitter. My advice? Do it perfectly.

Ingredients

  • Pasta
  • Garlic, thinly sliced
  • Olive oil
  • Parmigiano-reggiano cheese
  • Chopped fresh parsley
  • Red pepper flakes
  • Coarse black pepper

Steps

  1. Bring a pot of water to a boil.
  2. Salt the water throughly before adding your pasta.
  3. Boil your pasta to 'just' under the package instructions, make sure to reserve some of the pasta water.
  4. Combine garlic and olive oil in a cold skillet.
  5. Cook over medium heat to slowly toast garlic, about 10 minutes.
  6. Cook and stir until garlic is golden brown, about another 5 minutes.
  7. Remove from heat.
  8. Stir red pepper flakes, black pepper, and salt into the pasta.
  9. Pour in olive oil and garlic, and sprinkle on Italian parsley and half of the Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese; stir until combined.
  10. Serve.
